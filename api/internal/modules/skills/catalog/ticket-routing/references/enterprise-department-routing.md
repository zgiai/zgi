# Enterprise Department Routing

## Use When

Use when an employee, customer, partner, merchant, or external stakeholder asks a question that should be routed to an enterprise department.

## Department Mapping Heuristics

- Finance: invoices, payments, reimbursements, receipts, account reconciliation, fees, refunds, billing records.
- Legal: contracts, disputes, breach, liability, claims, legal notices, compliance interpretation.
- Leasing or business development: merchant entry,招商, lease consultation, shop operation, partnership, space inquiry.
- Property operations: facility service, environment, security, parking, maintenance, public area, tenant operations.
- IT: system login, account permission, device, network, software, data access, platform fault.
- Administration: office supplies, meeting rooms, workplace service, vehicle, reception, logistics, general admin support.
- HR: onboarding, attendance, payroll policy, benefits, employee certificate, organization procedure.
- Customer service: general consultation, complaint intake, customer follow-up, service coordination.

## Primary Owner Rules

- Select the team that can make or coordinate the next actionable decision.
- Add collaboration teams only when they clearly need to provide evidence, approval, repair, or response.
- If the issue requests policy interpretation plus execution, route primary ownership to the policy owner and list execution team as collaborator.

## Risk Rules

- Legal/financial claims, public complaint escalation, media exposure, safety incidents, and contract deadlines should be flagged for manual confirmation.

## Clarification Rules

Ask when issue wording only names a broad department but lacks the actual problem or desired outcome.
