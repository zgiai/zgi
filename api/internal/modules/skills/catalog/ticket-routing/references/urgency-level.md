# Urgency Level

## Use When

Use when the main task is determining urgency, escalation, priority, or response level.

## Levels

### High

Use high urgency when the issue involves:

- Personal safety, fire, flood, electrical hazard, elevator trapped person, security threat, or medical risk.
- Business-critical outage, broad customer impact, payment deadline, legal deadline, public complaint escalation, or major reputation risk.
- Ongoing property damage, service interruption affecting many people, or time-sensitive contractual consequence.

### Medium

Use medium urgency when:

- The issue affects normal work or living experience but has no immediate safety risk.
- A customer complaint requires coordinated follow-up.
- A payment, invoice, contract, or system issue has a near-term deadline.
- Multiple teams may need to collaborate.

### Low

Use low urgency when:

- The request is general consultation, routine information update, non-urgent administrative support, or non-critical service request.
- No safety, deadline, financial loss, legal exposure, or broad impact is indicated.

## Manual Confirmation

Mark `需人工确认` when:

- Safety or legal risk is possible but not confirmed.
- Scope, deadline, or affected people are unclear.
- The issue is emotionally escalated or alleges misconduct.
- The user requests a specific priority but source facts do not support it.

## Output Rule

Always include the reason for urgency level and missing facts that could change the level.
